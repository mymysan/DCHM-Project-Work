The Danish Exhibition Building

A competition was held preceeding the Baltic exhibition in Denmark where prominent Danish architects competed for signifcant money prices for the privelege of representing Denmark. Henning Hansen took the price and designed the building with a mansion in danish zealand style. It had red walls and a red brick roof. It's midship had a large renassance gate, preceeded by a bridge traversing a waterfilled moat. The large renessance gate was carved out from sandstone transported from the island of Bornholm off the coast of southwestern Sweden. The danish exhibiton building was a two floor house with a brick facade, and with windows of small square design. It's design was very symbolic for the jugend architectural and art style in the beginning of the 20th century.


During the daily life of the Baltic Exhibition the construction on the danish building was briefly halted as lumber master Nielsen of Denmark purportedly paid his workers nearly half of what the Swedish construction union had found reasonable. A conflict later to be resolved by succumbing to Swedish labor law as the exhibiton was, albeit very close to Denmark, on Swedish soil.

The last manifestation of the Jugend architectural & art style in Sweden

The Baltic Exhibition was an art industry and craftsman event between the 15th of May and the 4th of October 1914. The exhibiton took place in Malmo, Sweden. Four nations participated in the event: Sweden, Denmark, Germany and Russia. The participating countries exhibited buildings among other - built and torn down solely for the event manifesting the Jugend architectural and art style within Sweden, for the last time. The exhibiton was a friendly yet competetive event where each country sought to show their dignity by showcasing their cultural contribution and local papers fierying up the sense of national dignity.
The jugend style was present from 1890 to the 1920s, signified by a reaction against the historicistic thinking. The style was a mix of several art flows; symbolized by assymetries, meandering plant and flower lines with a naturalistic ornamentic (Jugend, 2023). The Baltic exhibition represented the later Jugend style that differed from the earlier interations with more straight lines and geometric shapes (Jugens / art nouveau - design - och stilhistoria under modern tid 1800-tal, 2023)
The exhibiton in size measured 51.6 hectare. It was the biggest exhibiton hosted in the nordic countries at the time, double the size of the Stockholm exhibiton of 1897.


Revisiting history, this website was built as part of a minor digitization project to once again illuminate an extrordinary event bringing nations together commonly fighting wars against one another thoughout history. This time gathered at peace under the communion of cultural aspirations - exhibiting creativity, not destruction.

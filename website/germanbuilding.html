The German Exhibition Building

The jugend architectural symbolic features could be seen in the German exhibiton building's stylish arcades around the central yard. The building and contribution of Germany was done by Hans Alfred Richter. The central yard in its jugend style had a fountain made of majolica to which blueprints had been made by the Swedish architects Alfred Grenander in Berlin. The building was a two-floor building in the Wertheim style with Manzard roof, measuring 75 metres in length and 15 metres in width. The building had two differing sideships with vestibules, arcades and stairs to the second floor. The midship hosted an exhibition of articles from Emperor Wilhems castle in the Cadine, existing of plates, pillars and more in majolica.


The contribution of Germany was said to have been granted a stunning 100 000 mark at the time and totalled 16000 square metres. Construction work at the exhibiton was dire, a construction worker died falling 16 metres and receiving 16 beams on top of the body during work at the German building. At the 7th of May 1914 a German warship picked up all Germans called home to serve in the first world war.
